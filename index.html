Project Abstract

Importance of deep ocean observations by deep floats and Deep NINJA

The importance of deep ocean observation has been gradually recognized to understand oceanic effects on anthropogenic climate change accurately, specially the oceanic heat content and the sea level rise. To make such observations, deep floats are among the most suitable devices to observe deep ocean at reasonable cost; a monitoring network with many deep floats, "deep Argo", is urgently needed. However, there have been no such floats available for waters below 2,000 m depth,　and now some of universities, institutes, and float makers are developing such deep floats internationally.
The Japan Agency for Marine-Earth Science and Technology (JAMSTEC) and Tsurumi-Seiki Co. Ltd. (TSK) began to develop a deep float in 2010, and finally succeeded with "Deep NINJA" in April 2013 (Figure 1). Deep NINJA can observe the ocean at depths up to 4,000 m globally, except for those seas covered by ice throughout the year. Figure 2 shows a schematic view of a Deep NINJA operation in the ocean compared to a normal (Argo) float.

Deep ocean observations by Deep NINJA floats operated by JAMSTEC

Until now, JAMSTEC, in collaboration with TSK, has deployed 14 Deep NINJA floats (including 2 prototypes) mainly in the Southern Ocean. As of the end of May 2014, the floats have operated well and have observed more than 180 oceanic profiles. Notably, one Deep NINJA float (S/N 6) deployed off the Adelie Coast of Antarctica measured deep profiles under sea ice throughout an Antarctic winter and continued to observe seasonal changes of the deep/bottom waters for more than one year.This website introduces the Deep NINJA float itself (some photos at deployment, Figures 3 and 4) and the inventory of the Deep NINJA observations, e.g., date and location of float deployment, and observation parameters such as observation cycle and profile depth. Also, there are several figures on the observation results, e.g., trajectories of float movements, and temporal changes of temperature and salinity profiles measured by floats.

Data

Release of scientifically quality-controlled data of Deep NINJA observations

The observations with Deep NINJA floats are carried out under Japan Argo. We here prepare a product of a quality-controlled dataset of Deep NINJA observations for convenient use on scientific/educational purposes. The quality-control was led by JAMSTEC on the basis of mainly comparisons with highly accurate shipboard CTD observations conducted at float deployments. For the accesses of the data, please contact us after the confirmation of the below Terms and Conditions.
All raw and officially quality-controlled data of the Deep NINJA observations will plan to be available to the public via the Argo Global Data Archive Centers (GDACs) in the near future, probably under the same framework of international Argo. Note that the raw measurements of Deep NINJA still include some issues to be resolved and examined on their quality as follows:

  1. The CTD sensor on Deep NINJA, which is the current standard model for deep floats, was developed originally for "Argo floats," which work in waters shallower than 2,000 m depth. Its performance under high pressure in the deep ocean was not examined sufficiently.
  2. Temperature and salinity measurements of the CTD sensor seem to have a bias which depends on pressure. JAMSTEC has found that salinity measurements of Deep NINJA tend to be fresher at higher pressure.
  3. International Argo has no agreement on the criteria for data quality for measurements at deeper than 2,000 dbar depth. Thus, there are no standard for quality control for deep observations.
